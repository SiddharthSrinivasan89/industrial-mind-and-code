# Agentic Bullwhip Experiment — Results Analysis
**Date:** 2026-02-27 08:05
**Branch:** Agentic-bullwhip-experiment/Version_4
**Grand total cost:** $15.23

---

## Experiment Design

2×2 factorial: **blind vs context** × **lightweight (gpt-4.1-mini) vs reasoning (o1)**
4 configs × 3 runs × 12 ordering periods × 3 tiers = 432 LLM calls
Supply chain: OEM → Ancillary → Component (serial, each tier run by an LLM agent)

---

## OVAR Results (Primary Metric)

OVAR = Var(orders) / Var(demands) over periods 1–12. Values > 1 indicate bullwhip amplification. Lower is better.

| Config | OEM | Ancillary | Component | OEM CV% |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| blind_lightweight | 2.247 ± 0.017 | 3.007 ± 0.148 | 4.374 ± 0.140 | 0.76% |
| blind_reasoning | 2.239 ± 0.017 | 2.944 ± 0.020 | 4.268 ± 0.043 | 0.77% |
| context_lightweight | 2.235 ± 0.003 | 2.481 ± 0.527 | 3.271 ± 0.501 | 0.14% |
| context_reasoning | **8.679 ± 3.039** | 3.350 ± 0.438 | 2.744 ± 1.025 | **35.02%** |

---

## Key Findings

### 1. All four configs confirm the bullwhip effect
Every OVAR > 1 at every tier across all configs. The cascade pattern — not whether it exists — is what differs between treatments.

### 2. Blind configs show the textbook upstream cascade
Both blind configs follow the classic pattern: OEM (~2.24) → Ancillary (~2.97) → Component (~4.32), with each upstream tier amplifying more. gpt-4.1-mini and o1 produce nearly identical OVAR values in the blind treatment, confirming that model capability alone does not reduce bullwhip without additional context.

### 3. Context + gpt-4.1-mini partially dampens the cascade
Downstream tiers improve meaningfully: ancillary drops from 3.007 to 2.481 (−17%), component from 4.374 to 3.271 (−25%). OEM is virtually unchanged. The improvement is noisy — ancillary CV of 21% indicates inconsistent benefit across runs, suggesting the lightweight model only sometimes acts on the context effectively.

### 4. Context + o1 creates an anomalous inverted cascade
The most striking finding. OEM OVAR explodes to 8.679 ± 3.039 (CV 35%), while component OVAR drops to 2.744. The cascade completely inverts. o1 appears to over-interpret seasonal market signals at the OEM tier — its peak overshoot reached 1.72× max demand vs ~1.22× for all other OEM tiers. This volatility at the top of the chain propagates and amplifies downstream in ABSOLUTE order volume, but each tier's OVAR relative to its own received demand decreases as they smooth the signal.

### 5. Pattern score = 0.0 (keyword sub-score) — metric redesigned
Inspection of raw reasoning text at event periods confirms models reason arithmetically — they calculate order quantities from backlog + demand without naming seasonal events. No festival keyword appeared in any of the 432 calls.

The pattern_score metric has been redesigned for future runs:
- **keyword_score**: fraction of distinct seasonal keywords found (list expanded to include `dussehra`, `navratri`, `deepawali`, `festival`, `seasonal`, `peak`, `anticipat`, etc.)
- **elevation_score**: fraction of (tier × event_period) pairs where order ≥ 110% of the tier's mean non-event-period order — captures seasonal responsiveness even when models don't verbalise it
- **pattern_score**: mean of the two sub-scores

This run's results cannot be retroactively re-scored with the elevation metric.

## Data Quality Note

gpt-4.1-mini occasionally formatted numbers with comma-separators (e.g. `27,959` instead of `27959`), causing JSON parse failures. Affected calls defaulted to `order_quantity = 0`. This would have suppressed some orders and slightly deflated OVAR for both lightweight configs. The true lightweight OVAR values may be marginally higher.

## Summary Verdict

| Verdict | Config |
|---|---|
| Best bullwhip reduction | **context_lightweight** — lower downstream OVAR, consistent OEM, $0.007/run |
| Most dangerous combination | **context_reasoning** — o1 over-interprets context, destabilises OEM tier, massive over-ordering |
| Most consistent behaviour | **blind_reasoning** — lowest CV% across all tiers (all below 1%) |

### Recommended follow-up
1. Fix gpt-4.1-mini JSON parse errors (prompt engineering or JSON mode)
2. Investigate why pattern score = 0 — examine raw reasoning text from context runs
3. Consider re-running context_reasoning with a temperature cap or order-smoothing instruction to test whether o1's OEM volatility can be constrained
4. The context prompt may need explicit anti-overreaction guidance for reasoning models
